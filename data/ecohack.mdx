The **EcoHack dataset** (`ecohack_boston_environment.txt`) is a synthetic environmental quality report for Boston, Q3 2025. It contains detailed metrics on air quality, urban heat islands, water quality, waste management, climate resilience, and environmental justice impacts.

## Dataset overview

<Info>
**File**: `data/ecohack_boston_environment.txt`  
**Scope**: City of Boston Environmental Quality Report, Q3 2025  
**Size**: ~20 lines, 8,100+ characters  
**Format**: Plain text, structured report
</Info>

## Data sections

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Air quality">
    ### Air quality summary
    
    - Boston's AQI averaged **42 (Good)** during Q3 2025, a 7% improvement over Q3 2024
    - 12 days exceeded the "Moderate" threshold (AQI > 100), concentrated in July heat wave
    - **Roxbury** monitoring station: 15.3 µg/m³ PM2.5 (28% above citywide average)
    - **East Boston**: 22 ppb NO2 near airport corridor (citywide avg: 14 ppb)
    
    <Info>
    The data explicitly shows environmental justice communities face disproportionate air quality impacts.
    </Info>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Heat islands">
    ### Urban heat island effect
    
    - Neighborhoods with &lt;15% tree canopy: **8-12°F hotter** than 30%+ canopy areas
    - Most affected: Dorchester (11% canopy), Mattapan (13%), East Boston (9%)
    - City goal: 35% canopy by 2035 requires planting ~120,000 new trees
    
    **Key insight**: Tree canopy coverage directly correlates with surface temperature disparities.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Water quality">
    ### Water quality and CSO events
    
    - Boston Harbor met EPA standards on **94% of testing days** (up from 89% in 2024)
    - Combined sewer overflow (CSO) events increased **15%** due to August storms
    - CSO discharge near Dorchester Bay and Charles River showed elevated E. coli for 2-4 days post-event
    - Deer Island Treatment Plant: 370M gallons/day average, 1.2B gallons peak during storms
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Waste & climate">
    ### Waste, recycling, and climate
    
    **Recycling metrics:**
    - 28% contamination rate (plastic bags, food-soiled materials)
    - 24% waste diverted from landfills
    - Mattapan and Hyde Park: lowest participation (18% and 21%)
    - Jamaica Plain composting pilot: 45% household participation
    
    **Climate resilience:**
    - 1.5 feet sea level rise projected by 2050
    - $80 billion in property at risk (East Boston, Seaport, Dorchester waterfront)
    - 3 of 12 coastal resilience projects completed
    - 47 miles of adapted shoreline planned, 8% completed
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Environmental justice">
    ### Environmental justice metrics
    
    Communities designated as EJ populations experienced:
    
    - **35% more days** with poor air quality
    - **2.4x higher** childhood asthma hospitalization rates
    - **40% less** green space access per capita
    - **3.1x higher** likelihood of living near contaminated sites
    
    **City response**: $12M allocated in FY2025 for EJ community improvements (60% increase over FY2024)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Querying from code

Here's how the RAG pipeline loads and queries this dataset:

```python
from llama_index.core import VectorStoreIndex, SimpleDirectoryReader, Settings
from llama_index.llms.ollama import Ollama
from llama_index.embeddings.huggingface import HuggingFaceEmbedding

# Configure local AI
Settings.llm = Ollama(model="llama3.1", request_timeout=120.0)
Settings.embed_model = HuggingFaceEmbedding(model_name="all-MiniLM-L6-v2")

# Load the EcoHack dataset
data_file = "data/ecohack_boston_environment.txt"
documents = SimpleDirectoryReader(input_files=[data_file]).load_data()

# Build vector index
index = VectorStoreIndex.from_documents(documents)
query_engine = index.as_query_engine(streaming=True, similarity_top_k=3)

# Query
response = query_engine.query(
    "Which Boston neighborhoods have the worst air quality and why?"
)
response.print_response_stream()
```
